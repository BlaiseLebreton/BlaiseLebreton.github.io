Projects

2019 - 2021

ADAS
Autonomous Car

Yvelines Institute of Science and Technology

In pair, we designed and developed an autonomous vehicle based on a radio-controlled car. The goal was to make the car follow a track based solely on video processing. We also wanted it to be able to map its environment (using a LIDAR) and to avoid any obstacle.
We added multiple sensors such as a camera, a LIDAR, an ultrasonic sensor… For the processing unit, we used a Raspberry Pi 3 coupled with an Arduino Mega2560 for the input/output management.

2019

PA-10
Industrial Robot Arm

Yvelines Institute of Science and Technology

The aim of this project was to make a robotic arm sort foam balls by color, based on image processing. We interacted with the robot through a version of Linux RTAI.
I was the project manager and had to oversee a team of twelve colleagues from mechanical, software and electrical engineering backgrounds.

2018

Autonomous Container Crane

Yvelines Institute of Science and Technology - Paul Bert Secondary School

I oversaw a team of five eighth graders who were participating in a competition where they had to conceive an autonomous container crane. The robot was made using the LEGO Mindstorm base. I helped them along the project by providing technical advice. They learned to program the robot using a version of LabVIEW.
